This was a very valuable experience as it was the first time we had to work in a team to complete a serious task given to us and under a short time frame (about 4 hours including our lunch). It helped me get used to working under pressure and also at looking and understanding each others code as there were many times where one of us would be stuck on something and we'd share our screens to show our code so that others could give solutions to the problems.

This was the final project that we had to do during the last week of the 6 week coding boot camp, it was to create a task planner which could add and delete tasks as well as saving them to the browser's local storage so that when you refresh the page or exit and come back to it, all the data will still be there. In the end as you can see in the image above, I managed to complete the task and it works, you can create your own task by inputting information into each field, which will then take the data through a validation check to make sure the fields aren't empty and to also check if the user has reached over a certain amount of characters as a minimum. You can then refresh the page and the cards will still be displayed, when you click on the delete button it will delete that card from the screen but it also deletes it from local storage and from the array containing all the different tasks in my JavaScript code.

Anime

Naruto

Naruto was the first anime that I started watching, I never knew about it or about anime in general but I played the games that they made based on it and around the age of 15 I was interested and began to watch it.

I can easily say now that it is one of my favourites of all time, watching the characters' journey from the beginning to the end was such an emotional journey and I feel nostalgic whenever I think about it. Despite it being an anime and not real, it has many important life lessons and, in my opinion, it is also one of the very few anime that has good soundtracks, I still listen to some of them to this day.

Death Note

This was recommended to me by a friend and I'm so glad that he told me to watch it, it's a classic that I'd recommend to anyone even if they're not that into anime (I heard the English voice acting on it is really good too but I'd still recommend it in Japanese with subtitles).

This anime is of a detective genre and had my heart racing till the very end, this is definitely a gem in the sense it's really unique and the storyline is really well thought of, its not predictable like the anime nowadays and always has you wanting to watch the next episode to find out what will happen next.

Games

Zone Of The Enders

This was the first game that I properly got into after growing up from being a baby and being able to finally understand and read words so I could actually complete a game. It will always be my most favourite game even compared to today's high graphic games.

The concepts of mechs and technology in the game really fascinated me along with the aspect of new energy sources and colonising other planets, it is one of the very few games that I went back to complete again and again, and even now I after experiencing today's types of games, I'd gladly go back and play it all over again.

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice

In this game, you play as a samurai who has to defeat enemies and bosses in order to progress through the story. I find the combat system in this game really fun, you actually require skill and practice to get past something; you cannot just mash buttons and hope for the best like most combat games these days, and so you get a really good feeling of satisfaction when you finally get past something.

The art in this game is also really amazing as well as the voice acting and animation in both the English and Japanese playthroughs.
